Usability Session Notes — Amazon Shopping App (Mobile)
Task: Find and purchase a specific product within a budget
Observer: Discovery Lens team | Date: April 2026
Participant: P1, female, 28, occasional online shopper

---

Task 1: Find wireless headphones under £50

- P1 opens app, types "wireless headphones" in search bar immediately — no hesitation
- 1st results page: all sponsored. P1 says "are any of these not ads?"
- P1 scrolls past first 6 results without clicking — visually skipping sponsored badges
- Clicks on 7th result (£44.99). Reads title but skips straight to reviews
- Sorts reviews by "most recent" — "I don't trust the top ones"
- Spends ~90 seconds reading reviews. Pauses on a 2-star: "this is the kind I care about"
- Notices "Fulfilled by Amazon" badge. "OK so at least returns will be easy"
- Tries to use "Compare with similar items" — taps it, waits, feature doesn't load
- P1: "I'll just open another tab" — exits feature, does manual comparison via back button
- Adds to cart. Proceeds to checkout.

Task 2: Apply a discount code

- P1 looks for discount field at checkout. Doesn't see it immediately
- Taps through 3 checkout screens before finding "Gift cards & promotional codes"
- "Why is it called that? I just have a discount code"
- Enters code. Error: "code not applicable to this item." No explanation of why.
- P1: "I guess I just won't use it." Completes purchase without code.
- Post-task comment: "I'd want to know before I add things to cart whether my code works."

Task 3: Find a previously viewed item

- P1 navigates to account > browsing history
- Scrolls through ~30 items. "I don't remember which one it was"
- Tries search with partial description. Doesn't find it.
- Gives up after 2 minutes. "It's probably gone. I'll just search again."

Observer notes:
- P1 relies heavily on reviews to compensate for low product trust
- Sponsored content creates friction and reduces trust in search results
- Discount code UX is buried and error messages are not actionable
- No easy way to retrieve or organise past browsing — history is a flat unfiltered list
